Volunteering

Volunteering in Thai Binh, Vietnam

General Information

So you are thinking about volunteering at the New World Center ? Here are a few things you should know. Our center is home to around 20 volunteers from all over the world. This is also the maximum amount of volunteers our center can accommodate at a time. Yes accommodate, if you decide to volunteer at our center you will be provided accommodation, food and compensation for your help. The compensation is a fixed amount of around 200 USD per month. It is important to note however, that we are not an NGO, we are a social enterprise and thus we pay tax and employ staff. For more detailed information about New World please refer to the about page.

Teaching

Teaching in New World is pretty straight forward. The volunteers of New World are tasked with helping vietnamese students with pronunciation and and getting acquainted expressing themselves through the use of the english language. Grammar itself is taught by vietnamese english teachers (some also hired by New World). While it is not your job to teach grammar, you are still allowed to incorporate that in your classes (depending of course which type of classes). Vietnamese teachers and volunteers teach english at the different New World centers across Thai Binh. Next to teaching at the New World centers themselves, the volunteers of New World are also hired by many state schools and private schools in Thai Binh to teach english to kids. So as a volunteer you may find yourself sometimes teaching at the New World center (s) or other times at different schools in different parts of Thai Binh.

Important to know

There are 4 New World centers spread across Thai Bin. Mrs. Hong is the director of the New World International English center which includes all of New World centers in Thai Binh. Mrs. Hong, her husband and two sons live a short distance away from Kien Xuong. Mrs. Hong is the go to person for any questions regarding your stay in Thai Binh, anything regarding your visa, your accommodation and also food. Mrs. Hong is also the one who is in charge of the teaching schedule. More information on the main center can be found on the about page

Mrs. Ly is like the right hand man of Mrs. Hong, she helps around with a multitude of tasks ranging from helping around in the center, assisting the volunteers with teaching and she also overseas some of the planning of classes that go on. In your stay you will also encounter Mis Ly’s Husband, Duc he is also the all around helper in the center he does not teach though he just helps with the maintenance of the center. Ms. Liene is charged with creating the lesson planning for secondary schools. Ms. Huong is charged with creating the lesson plans for primary schools and she does this with the help of Mis Mai who works at the New World English center in Diem Dien. Just like the other teaching staff, Ms. Huong helps as the vietnamese teaching assistant in some classes taught by volunteers. Finally, Mrs. Quey is charged with the lesson plans of kindergarten school. Furthermore she is also a vietnamese teaching assistant. It is important to note that almost all of the staff mentioned, teach English grammar to students, the volunteers job is to help with vocabulary, pronunciation and more importantly help vietnamese students to become acquainted to interacting through the english language.

First of all, Coordinator/manager of the second center is Ms. Mai. Ms. Mai lives in Vu Thu at the location of the second New World center. She has two daughters Minh Thu and Thang Thu. Ms. Mai is very hospitable and generous, she will guide you through your classes if you have to teach in Vu Thu. Secondly, Coordinator/manager for the third Center is Mrs. Vanh Anh. Sometimes you will also be teaching classes in this center, for any questions regarding your classes you can contact them.

Accomodation

In Thai Binh , we have 2 houses. Old House (old centre) has 8 bedrooms, spread across 3 floors. There is a Bathroom on every floor with showering facilities.The switch on the outside of each bathroom heats the water in advance. Within 30 mins you will have enough Hot water .The bedrooms are quite generous in size. A wardrobe, bed(pillows & duvet) and a desk live in the room. Each Bedroom has a full functioning balcony for relaxing,chillaxing or spruce up with some colors and flowers, totally up to you. In the main house classes can be held during the day,in the evenings and on weekends. So there are 2 classrooms conjoining off the kitchen downstairs. Fully equipped with everything needed (ie. Tables,chairs,projector and audio leads.) In the second classroom the entrance to the back garden is located. It's quite a spacious area & enclosed . It's nice to bring your kindergartners for a bit of a refresh between classes. There are also 2 classrooms on the 4th Floor upstairs. Again fully equipped with everything necessary. The lobby area downstairs is quite big with our dining table, the reception & a seating area for the kids and parents when needed.

The second house is also the main building of the New World International center. Most of you will be staying in this building (if there is space left). You will also be giving teaching some of your english classes in this building. The building is a story building that houses 6 classrooms, one large kitchen, a balcony on the 5th and 6th floor, one public bathroom on each of the floors except for the 5th and the 6th - On these floor there is one bathroom in the double rooms-, four double rooms - these are shared rooms in which two people can stay-, 4 single bedrooms and one common room on the 4th floor. All of the single rooms are equipped with a king size bed a desk and a wardrobe. All of the bedroom have two of each (wardrobe, desk and kingsize bed). Two of the 4 single rooms are spacious rooms and the other two are fairly sized but. There is also airconditioning in all of the rooms, but some rooms share on 1 airco (4 rooms in total). There is also a common laundry room, the laundry rooms has a washing machine but no dryer. So all of the clothes are air/sundried on the 6th floor on racks.

Food

New World provides food for its volunteers twice a day everyday. Lunch is served around 11:00 am day everyday. And dinner around 6:30pm everyday. The menu is always vietnamese dishes, but if you would like you can also cook some food yourself as some volunteers also do. Meat is generally served, but if your a vegetarian, food will be prepared for you as well just let us know beforehand so that the cook takes you into consideration. Food is for the most part eaten together, in the main house (the main center) we eat together and we also clean up together. Sometimes volunteers chose to eat out or eat with the families of their students, this is completely fine, but it is very much appreciated if the volunteer lets the staff know that they are not going to partake in the meals as to reduce the wastage of food.

Travel

With your stay here in Thai Binh, you will get chances to travel. Travel is mostly done during the weekend because that's when all of us are free. You can choose to travel around and explore by yourself. However, sometimes trips are planned by the volunteers here. Many tourist areas like for example Ninh Binh (1,5 h from Thai Binh), Cat Ba (3,5 h from Thai Binh) or Hanoi (2 h from Thai Binh). There are many things to see and places to visit. The center has around three bikes and you many volunteers just borrow the bikes to go exploring around in or outside of Thai Binh

Guidelines

First of all there are some rules we follow at the house, if you get accepted to teach in New World. The main rule is respect for and tolerance of others. This rule is not written down, it’s just common sense and a matter of ethics. If you are unable to abide to this rule, be prepared to not have many friends. Another rule of the house is to clean up after yourself, we try to help Mrs. Hong have keep the place clean by helping with washing dishes after food. Another thing that you need to consider, if you get accepted to volunteer in New World is to save energy. Electricity is becoming increasingly expensive in Thai binh, therefore we have to be mindful about our energy consumption. Thus, turn lights off if your not using, turn of the water heater when your done using it or if you see a some lights on where there are nobody around, turn it of.

Next to house rules, there are also some rules we need to follow when teaching in New World. First of all there is a dress code we have to follow when teaching at state schools. No sleeveless shirts, short pants (over the knee) or slippers, you can wear shirts that have sleeves and pants that are under the knees or long pants. If your wearing dresses these you be below the knee and preferably worn over some leggings. Other then that, if you are sick you have to let the staff know beforehand so that they can find a replacement for you. If you do not show up for your shift more than three times your contract will be ended and you will be sent back to your home country. When traveling around be sure to always wear a helmet and drive save. Traffic accidents are common is vietnam, on average one person dies every hour.
