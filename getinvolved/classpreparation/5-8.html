Age group 5 - 8

Preparation

Classes for this age group usually have a duration of around 30-45 min. However in the new world centers the classes are around 1:30 h. These classes are taught in the same format as the kids of age group 2-4 but there are changes in what and how the classes are taught. In the 1:30 h classes we course material from english books e.g. Family and Friends 2-6 (author Naomi Simmons). And in the 45 min classes we use course material from the english books Tieng Anh (4-8) by authors Bo Giao Duc and Va Dao Tao.

Warm-up

With these kids as warm up some physical exercise is also done, along with singing songs. What is added is a round of questions, where some basic english questions and answers are taught to students for example about what time is it?, what the is the date ? or how are you feeling?. Along with the questions some answers to those questions are also taught to the students. In the 1:30 classes, usually more time is spent warming-up or even incorporated during teaching as a break.

Teaching

After the warm-up round the teacher then will move on to the the teaching part of the class. Where just like in some lower age-groups flashcards are taught as well. Again, flashcards will be provided beforehand by the vietnamese teachers at the new world center, so the volunteer does not have to prepare those. The volunteer can also incorporate different ways of teaching the flashcards then just repetition, like for example through games (see list of games). The 1:30h classes are based on the content of the english material (books) that is provided by the center. In the 1:30 h classes, more games are incorporated in the classes for example quizzing the kids on the flashcards (e.g. asking them to describe the classes), or creating and reciting poems during class related to the topic but also singing songs.

Games

The 30-45 minutes classes usually end with game playing (see list of games) and ending the class with goodbye song. In the 1:30 h classes games and songs are incorporated throughout the class so it is up to the volunteer on how she is going to teach the class.
